# Recommendations — Tyler Granlund Portfolio Site

## Context
This research was conducted to support the granlund-grove personal portfolio site (tylergranlund.com). The site already features a /ventures page, /work page, and hero section. The goal is to determine how Tyler's public speaking and professional content should be represented.

---

## Priority 1: Create a "Speaking" or "Talks" Section/Page

### Rationale
The Adobe "Learn from your peers" presentation is a significant credential:
- Tyler was the **inaugural presenter** for this Adobe Commerce initiative
- He is called an "Adobe Commerce expert and advocate" and "champion" by Adobe
- The event is hosted on Adobe's official Experience League platform
- A full transcript, slides, and recording are publicly available

### Implementation Options

**Option A: Dedicated /speaking route**
- Single page listing all talks (current and future)
- Featured card for the Adobe presentation with:
  - Title, date, and event series
  - Embedded link to Experience League recording
  - "Download slides" link
  - Key topics covered (3 bullets)
  - Quote from the talk

**Option B: Add to existing /ventures page**
- Since /ventures already showcases "creative + field work," speaking could fit as "knowledge sharing"
- Less prominent than a dedicated page

**Option C: Home page mention**
- Brief stat or mention in the hero or career section
- Example: "Inaugural presenter, Adobe Learn from Your Peers series"

**Recommended: Option A** — A dedicated /speaking page is justified even with one major talk because:
1. It signals active thought leadership
2. It provides SEO value for "Tyler Granlund Adobe Commerce" queries
3. It creates room for future talks
4. It can link back to the portfolio's case studies (e.g., the e-commerce systems he's built)
