# Brad Frost — Speaking Page Pattern (bradfrost.com/speaking)

**Captured:** 2026, headless browse  
**Tier:** 2 (recognized industry expert, primary source on own page)

## IA Structure
1. Hero line: "I like to talk." (one-liner positioning)
2. Topical intent paragraph: "I'm passionate about the web, creativity, design systems, music, and life, and love speaking at high-quality conferences that share that passion, have **diverse line-ups** and value creating a positive, creative, and safe community."
3. CTA: "If you'd like me to speak at your event, please **get in touch**."
4. Two tables: **Upcoming Events** | **Past Events**

## Table columns
- Upcoming: Event | Location | Date
- Past: Event | Location | Date | Materials (Video / Slides / Post badges per row)

## Volume
~200+ past events from 2011 → 2026. Table is the primary IA — no card grid, no fancy filters. Year is part of the date column.

## What's missing (deliberately)
- No talk abstracts on this page (abstracts live on individual /blog posts referenced via "Post" link)
- No headshots, no logos, no testimonial carousel
- No booking form embedded — single "get in touch" link routes to /contact

## Why this works for an exec audience
- **Scannable**: a recruiter or conference organizer can see range + cadence in 5 seconds
- **Provenance**: every claim is linkable (Video/Slides/Post)
- **Honesty filter**: empty Upcoming table is acceptable; the depth of Past Events does the credibility work
- **Speaker booker UX**: they need date, venue, topic — exactly what the table shows
